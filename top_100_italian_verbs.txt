The Imperfect Tense (imperfetto):
=============================================================================
-ARE (-avo, avi, ava, avamo, avate, avano)
-ERE (-evo, evi, eva, evamo, evate, evano)
-IRE (-ivo, ivi, iva, ivamo, ivate, ivano)

Use the Italian imperfect tense:
To describe what things were like, what people were doing and how people felt in the past:
It was hot.                    = Faceva caldo.
They were waiting impatiently. = Aspettavano impazienti.
We were all happy.             = Eravamo tutti felici.
I was hungry.                  = Avevo fame.

To say what people knew, thought or meant in the past:
I didn’t know what you meant. = Non sapevo cosa volevi dire.
I thought it was him.         = Pensavo che fosse lui.

To say what used to happen or what people used to do in the past:
We met every Friday. = Ci trovavamo ogni venerdì.
They used to sell eggs in the market. = Vendevano le uova al mercato.

To describe what was going on when an event took place:
We were watching the match when he came in. = Guardavamo la partita quando è entrato lui.	
It happened while they were asleep. = È successo mentre dormivano.
While you were talking I remembered something. = 
Mentre parlavi mi sono ricordato di qualcosa.	

- The imperfetto is commonly used for events that happened continuously in the past on a regular basis (e.g. “used to” in English):

When I was a kid in Italy, I used to eat pasta every day!
Quando ero un bambino in Italia, mangiavo la pasta tutti i giorni.

The Perfect Tense (passato prossimo):
=============================================================================
passato prossimo is usually made with avere (ho, hai, ha, abbiamo, avete, hanno) + verb.

-ARE (-> ato)
imparare  (to learn) — imparato
camminare (to walk)  — camminato

-ERE (-> uto)
credere (to believe) — creduto
tenere  (to keep)    — tenuto

-IRE (-> ito)
capire (to understand) — capito
finire (to finish)     — finito

Impersonal verbs:
=============================================================================
piovere (to rain) and nevicare (to snow) are always impersonal.
Piove. = It's raining.
Sta piovendo? = Is it raining?

parere and sembrare (to seem) are often used impersonally.
Pare di sì. = It seems so.
Pare di no. = Apparently not.
to appear   = parere (parso)

Examples:

Occorre farlo subito. = It should be done at once.
Non importa. = It doesn't matter.

The Infinitive:
=============================================================================
after adjectives and nouns that are followed by di:
Sono contento di vederti. = I'm glad to see you.
Sono stufo di studiare.   = I'm fed up of studying.

after another verb:
Posso entrare?            = Can I come in?
Cosa ti piacerebbe fare?  = What would you like to do?

to give instructions (signs, forms, recipes):
Rallentare   Slow Down
Spingere     Push

to tell someone (tu) not to do something:
Non toccarlo. = Don't touch it!

In Italian prepositions are followed by the infinitive:
Prima di aprire il... = Before opening the...

In Italian the infinitive, not the gerund, is used as a noun:
Ascoltare la musica è rilassante. = Listening to music is relaxing.
Camminare fa bene.  = Walking is good for you.
Mi piace cavalcare. = I like riding.

In Italian only the infinitive can follow another verb:
Volete aspettare? = Do you want to wait?
Desiderava migliorare il suo inglese. = He wanted to improve his English.
Bisogna prenotare. = You need to book.
Preferisco non parlarne. = I prefer not to talk about it.
